Gertrude's Story

1928 2004

Macon, Georgia

The "Scott Family Tree" began growing when Helen Gertrude Johnson was born September 08, 1928, in Macon, Georgia, to Bennie and Maime Johnson.  Gertrude was the younger of Bennie and Maime's two children.

Gertrude's parents, Bennie and Maime, passed away while Gertrude and her sister Mary Alice were young children.  As a consequence, Gertrude and Mary Alice were raised by their mother's sisters and brothers.  Gertrude by her Aunt Dorothy and Aunt Lee.  And Mary Alice, by her Uncle Bubba Lee.

After graduating grammar school, Gertrude decided to forgo high school, to began earning a living cleaning houses and picking cotton in nearby Thomson, Georgia.

When Gertrude became of age, she married Charlie Coleman Scott, taking on his surname and becoming Helen Gertrude Scott.  Gertrude and Charlie Coleman would go on to have six children together: Catherine, Peggy Ann, Charlie C. Scott Jr known as "Butchie", Mary Jane, Larry James and Gregory.

In 1958, Gertrude decided she had had enough of her marriage with Charlie Coleman, and separated herself from him.

Gertrude and her six children migrated north from Macon, Georgia to Chicago, Illinois;  Leaving Charlie Coleman behind in Macon.

Chicago, Illinois

In Chicago, Illinois Gertrude's family would grow.  She met Mr. Marshall Thompson, and the two of them had two children together: Anthony and Kathleen.

Marshall Thompson and Gertrude's relationship eventually fizzled out.  Moving on, Gertrude met Mr. Greene, and the two of them had one child together: Laura Ann.

Mr. Greene and Gertrude's relationship eventually fizzled out.  Moving on, Gertrude met Mr. Herbert Harris, and the two of them had one child together: Carolyn Marie.

Gertrude's family totaled ten children and she would go on to become a grandmother, great-grandmother, and great-great-grandmother many times over.

Gertrude became a member of Christ Deliverance Center Pentecostal Baptist Church.  As a member of CDC, Gertrude instilled strong moral values into her children and taught them about Jesus Christ.

In hindsight, Gertrude realized she had some unfinished business to attend to.  Having foregone high school to start earning a living, she had not earned her high school diploma.

To remedy this, Gertrude attended classes, studied hard, and did earn her high school diploma.

Gertrude passed away June 05, 2004 in Chicago, Illinois at age .  She left her spirit of goodwill, kindness, thoughtfulness, and doing unto others, as she would have others, do unto her; as a guiding light to all who knew her. 1

1 Gertrude's sister, Mary Alice, preceeded her in death, passing away in Atlanta, Georgia on November 11, 1998.

As You Write "Your Life Story" . . .

Think of your life as roll of undeveloped film.  Some photos will come out good, some not so good, and others just plain bad.  Portions of film not yet exposed are future life events that have not happened yet.

As much as we would like to, we cannot change "not so good" and "bad" life events, but we can take steps to ensure they don't happen again". 2

As we write our life stories, let us focus on positive happenings and accomplishesments - rather than unpleasant ones.

What I Had to Say . . .

I was influenced by the SCFI television series "Star Trek", which peaked my interest in Scientific theory and computers.  This coupled with the fact my best friend's older brother, Marvin, was a Computer Programmer, influenced me to become a Computer Technology Professional.

In 1980, while working as a Computer Professional at the University of Illinois, I met my wife to be Christine, who worked there too, as a Keypunch Operator.  We were married on Father's Day, June 21, 1981 at Christ Deliverance Church.  We both played on our department's softball team.  My wife couldn't catch (all that good) but she could hit.  The important thing is the two of us hit it off and our children and grandchildren are our history.

Later in 1980, my younger brother Anthony and my friend Alfred, were forming a band and needed a guitar player and a lead singer.  The two of them encouraged me to begin learning guitar (I did); and my wife Christine sang lead vocals.  I've been playing guitar every since and my wife still sings.

Anthony's Autobiography . . .

Playing Drums

My fascination for drums playing was a gift from God.  I began by playing the oatmeal and grits boxes, after mama cooked the oatmeal and grits.  I also played my bed springs after pulling back the mattress of my bed.

I later begin to play bongos and congos with Ray from 63rd street.  I continued playing the drums at Christ Deliverance Center church, on 63rd and Kimbark.

Later mama was able to buy me a drum set when we lived in 1132.  I went on to purchase my own set after the original set that mama got for me were run through by a taffy apple stick, I found left in the drums.  Then Alfred, my brother Greg and me began to form a group, in 1132, Apt. 901.

Education

My brother Butchie and I, attended Coyne American Institute together 1978 thru 1979 school year.  While there, I instructed Butchie and some of his classmates in electrical maintenance theory, because of my electrical/electronics major course of study at Martin Luther King high school, where I graduated in 1977.

I graduated from Coyne in 1979.  While in school at Coyne, I started working at downtown Chicago Marriott Hotel.  Where my official career as a Maintenance Engineer began, three months before graduating from Coyne.

Career

After years of working at the Chicago Marriott Hotel, my brother Butchie and me worked together at Neair, a non profit organization.  Where Butchie was very instrumental in aiding me in obtaining a job at Neair, as Electrical Maintenance Mechanic.

Later, I went on to attend ITT Technical Institute, where I graduated with my Electronic Engineering Technology degree in September of 1999.

After awhile, I was hired at Horseshoe Casino in Hammond, Indiana, as an Electronic Technician, and then on to Chicago Transit Authority as a Electrical Mechanic for 2200 Series trains up to the recent 5000 Series trains. Where I am now a retiree.

Ordainment

I became an ordained Elder in C.O.G.I.C. in 2009.
